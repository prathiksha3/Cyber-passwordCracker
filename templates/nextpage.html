Introduction

A brute force attack is a way for hackers to try and guess passwords or encryption keys by systematically trying every possible combination until the correct one is found. Imagine you have a lock with a combination code, and someone tries every possible number until the lock opens. That's essentially what a brute force attack does, but with passwords or other secure information.

Here's a simple breakdown:

Objective:

The hacker wants to gain access to a secured system (like a computer, website, or network).

Method:

They use a computer program to try all possible passwords or keys.

Time-Consuming:

It can take a very long time, especially if the password is long and complex.

Success:

Eventually, if given enough time and resources, the hacker can find the correct password or key.

Because brute force attacks can be very time-consuming, strong passwords (with a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters) and other security measures (like account lockout policies after multiple failed attempts) can help protect against them.
